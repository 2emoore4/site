simple led circuit
  • What is the purpose of the resistor?
    The voltage in the circuit drops across the resistor. Without the resistor, there would be more voltage to drop across the led. The led can't handle such a high voltage drop, so the resistor lowers it.
  • Using Ohm's Law (V=IR), what is the current running through this circuit?
    8.9 milliamps
  • Using Ohm's Law again, calculate the resistor value you would have to use in order to have only 15mA of current in this circuit?
    ~333.3 ohms
  • Assuming the LED drops 2.2V of voltage, how much voltage is dropped by the resistor?
    2.8 volts
simple led circuit with switch
  • Looking at the schematic diagram, if you moved the switch so that it was connected between the resistor and the LED, how would the circuit's behavior change?
    It wouldn't change. The circuit would still be incomplete when the switch is unpressed.
  • Looking at the schematic diagram, if you moved the switch so that it was connected between the LED and ground, how would the circuit's behavior change?
    No change, for the same reason.
simple led circuit with potentiometer
  • Why is the 560 ohm resistor necessary?
    If you turn the potentiometer all the way down, it would be as if there's nothing there. You would need a resistor to make up for the lack of resistance in the potentiometer. It prevents the led from frying.
  • How much current is flowing through the circuit when the 10kohm potentiometer is turned all the way up (offering the maximum resistance)?
    ~3.2 mA
  • What other types of variable resistors do you know about?
    rheostat
dueling leds circuit with potentiometer
  • Give a quick explanation of why this circuit behaves the way it does. Include an explanation for how the current going through each LED is affected by a single turn of the potentiometer.
    The potentiometer has three poles, so turning it will adjust which two poles have a higher or lower resistance. If the left two poles (or vice versa) have a lower resistance, then there will be a lower voltage drop on that side of the circuit and the led will be brighter.
capacitor charging circuit
  • How does adding a 2.2kohm resistor between the pushbutton and the capacitor affect the capacitor's charging behavior?
    The capacitor would simply take longer to fully charge.
capacitor discharging circuit with led decay
  • Looking at the circuit schematic diagram, why does the capacitor discharge up through the LED part of the circuit, and not directly from the capacitor down to ground?
    Electricity won't move from the capacitor to ground because there is no voltage drop in that part of the circuit. There is, however, a voltage drop in the part of the circuit with the resistor and the led, so that's where the electricity will flow.
